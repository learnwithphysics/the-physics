📖 Introduction

Everything around us moves because of forces. When we push or pull an object, its state of rest or motion changes. Sir Isaac Newton explained these changes using three important laws called Newton's Laws of Motion.

🎯 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand Force.
  • Learn Balanced & Unbalanced Forces.
  • Understand Newton's First Law.
  • Understand Newton's Second Law.
  • Understand Newton's Third Law.
  • Perform Virtual Activities.

💪 Force

A force is a push or a pull acting on an object. Force can change the speed, direction or shape of an object.

Examples

  • 🚪 Opening a door
  • ⚽ Kicking a football
  • 🛒 Pushing a shopping cart
  • 🏹 Pulling a bow

⚖ Balanced Forces

Balanced forces are equal in magnitude and opposite in direction. They do not change the motion of an object.

Example

Two students pulling a rope with equal force. The rope does not move.

➡ Unbalanced Forces

When forces are unequal, the object starts moving towards the greater force.

Example

In Tug of War, the stronger team wins.

🥇 Newton's First Law

An object continues to remain at rest or continues to move with uniform speed in a straight line unless an external unbalanced force acts on it. This law is also called the Law of Inertia.

Real Life Examples

  • 🚌 Passengers fall backward when a bus starts suddenly.
  • 🛑 Passengers move forward when a moving bus stops suddenly.
  • 🪙 Coin falls into the glass when the card is pulled quickly.

🥈 Newton's Second Law

The acceleration of an object depends on the applied force and its mass. Greater force produces greater acceleration, while greater mass produces less acceleration.

Formula

Force = Mass × Acceleration

F = m × a

SI Unit of Force = Newton (N)

🥉 Newton's Third Law

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. Whenever one object exerts a force on another object, the second object exerts an equal force in the opposite direction.

Examples

  • 🚀 Rocket launch
  • 🏊 Swimming
  • 🚣 Rowing a boat
  • 🚶 Walking

📚 Improve Your Learning

1. What is Force?

Force is a push or pull acting on an object.

2. What are Balanced Forces?

Balanced forces are equal and opposite. They do not change the motion of an object.

3. What are Unbalanced Forces?

Unbalanced forces change the state of motion of an object.

4. State Newton's First Law.

A body remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external unbalanced force.

5. What is Inertia?

Inertia is the tendency of a body to resist any change in its state of rest or motion.

6. State Newton's Second Law.

Force is equal to mass × acceleration (F = ma).

7. SI Unit of Force?

Newton (N)

8. State Newton's Third Law.

For every action there is an equal and opposite reaction.

9. Give one example of Newton's Third Law.

Rocket launch, Swimming, Walking or Rowing a boat.

10. Write the formula of Force.

F = m × a

  • ✅ Balanced forces do not change motion.
  • ✅ Unbalanced forces change motion.
  • ✅ Newton gave three laws of motion.
  • ✅ Force = Mass × Acceleration.
  • ✅ Every action has an equal and opposite reaction.
