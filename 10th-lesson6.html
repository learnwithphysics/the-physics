📖 Introduction

Everything around us is made of tiny particles called atoms. Atoms are the basic building blocks of matter.

🎯 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the structure of an atom.
  • Know protons, neutrons and electrons.
  • Learn important atomic models.
  • Understand atomic number and mass number.
  • Perform virtual activities.

⚛ What is an Atom?

An atom is the smallest particle of an element that retains all its chemical properties.

Important Facts

  • ⚛ Atoms are extremely small.
  • ⚛ They combine to form molecules.
  • ⚛ Every element is made of atoms.

🔬 Subatomic Particles

Particle Charge Location
Proton +1 Nucleus
Neutron 0 Nucleus
Electron -1 Outside Nucleus

📚 Atomic Models

Dalton's Atomic Theory

Matter consists of indivisible atoms.

Thomson Model

The atom is a positively charged sphere with electrons embedded in it.

Rutherford Model

The atom has a small, dense, positively charged nucleus with electrons revolving around it.

Bohr Model

Electrons revolve around the nucleus in fixed energy levels (shells).

🔢 Atomic Number (Z)

The number of protons present in the nucleus of an atom is called the Atomic Number.

Formula

Atomic Number (Z) = Number of Protons

Example: Hydrogen → Z = 1 Carbon → Z = 6 Oxygen → Z = 8

⚖ Mass Number (A)

The total number of protons and neutrons present in the nucleus is called the Mass Number.

Formula

Mass Number = Protons + Neutrons

Example: Carbon Protons = 6 Neutrons = 6 Mass Number = 12

🛰 Bohr's Atomic Model

  • ✅ Electrons revolve around the nucleus.
  • ✅ They move only in fixed energy levels (shells).
  • ✅ Electrons neither gain nor lose energy while revolving in a shell.
  • ✅ Energy is absorbed or released only when electrons jump between shells.

🧪 Isotopes

Atoms of the same element having the same atomic number but different mass numbers are called Isotopes.

Element Atomic No. Mass No.
Hydrogen (¹H) 1 1
Deuterium (²H) 1 2
Tritium (³H) 1 3

⚖ Isobars

Atoms having the same mass number but different atomic numbers are called Isobars.

Element Atomic No. Mass No.

Calcium 20 40

📚 Improve Your Learning

1. What is an atom?

2. Name the three subatomic particles.

Protons, Neutrons and Electrons.

3. What is Atomic Number?

Atomic Number is the number of protons present in the nucleus.

4. What is Mass Number?

Mass Number is the sum of protons and neutrons.

5. State Bohr's Atomic Model.

Electrons revolve around the nucleus in fixed energy levels called shells.

Atoms having the same atomic number but different mass numbers.

Electrons revolve around the nucleus in shells.

K shell can hold a maximum of 2 electrons.

10. What is a neutral atom?

A neutral atom has equal numbers of protons and electrons.

⭐ Important Definitions

  • ⚛ Atom – Smallest particle of an element.
  • 🔴 Proton – Positively charged particle.
  • ⚪ Neutron – Neutral particle.
  • 🔵 Electron – Negatively charged particle.
  • 🔢 Atomic Number = Number of Protons.
  • ⚖ Mass Number = Protons + Neutrons.
  • 🧪 Isotopes – Same Atomic Number, Different Mass Number.
  • ⚛ Isobars – Same Mass Number, Different Atomic Number.
