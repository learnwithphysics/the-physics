📖 Introduction

When light travels from one transparent medium to another, its speed changes. Because of this change in speed, the light ray bends. This bending of light is called refraction.

🎯 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand Refraction.
  • Learn Laws of Refraction.
  • Know Refractive Index.
  • Observe real-life examples.
  • Perform virtual experiments.

💡 What is Refraction?

Refraction is the bending of light when it passes from one transparent medium to another because its speed changes.

Daily Life Examples

  • 🥄 Spoon appears bent in water.
  • 🐟 Fish appears nearer than it actually is.
  • 🏊 Swimming pool appears shallow.
  • 💎 Diamonds shine due to refraction.

📜 Laws of Refraction

  • ✅ Incident ray, refracted ray and normal lie in the same plane.
  • ✅ Snell's Law is obeyed.

📐 Refractive Index

Refractive Index tells us how much light bends inside a medium.

Formula

n = c / v

  • n = Refractive Index
  • c = Speed of light in vacuum
  • v = Speed of light in the medium

📏 Snell's Law

Snell's Law gives the relationship between the angle of incidence and the angle of refraction.

Formula

n₁ sin i = n₂ sin r

  • i = Angle of Incidence
  • r = Angle of Refraction
  • n₁ = Refractive Index of Medium 1
  • n₂ = Refractive Index of Medium 2

📊 Refractive Index of Some Materials

Medium Refractive Index
Vacuum 1.00
Air 1.0003
Water 1.33
Ice 1.31
Glass 1.50
Diamond 2.42

➡ How Light Bends

Air ➜ Glass

Light slows down and bends towards the normal.

Glass ➜ Air

Light speeds up and bends away from the normal.

⭐ Important Points

  • ✅ Speed of light decreases in denser medium.
  • ✅ Frequency of light remains constant.
  • ✅ Wavelength changes during refraction.
  • ✅ Refraction occurs only when light changes medium.

📚 Improve Your Learning

1. What is refraction?

Refraction is the bending of light when it passes from one transparent medium to another due to a change in speed.

2. State Snell's Law.

For two given media, the ratio of sin i to sin r is constant.

3. What is Refractive Index?

It is the ratio of the speed of light in vacuum to the speed of light in the medium.

4. Write the formula for Refractive Index.

n = c / v

5. What happens when light enters a denser medium?

It bends towards the normal.

6. What happens when light enters a rarer medium?

It bends away from the normal.

7. Does frequency change during refraction?

No. Frequency remains constant.

8. Give two applications of refraction.

Spectacles, Camera, Microscope, Telescope.

9. Why does a swimming pool appear shallow?

Because of refraction of light.

10. Why does a pencil appear bent in water?

Because light rays bend when they pass from water to air.

⭐ Important Definitions

  • 💡 Refraction – Bending of light.
  • 📏 Normal – Imaginary line perpendicular to the surface.
  • 📐 Angle of Incidence – Angle between incident ray and normal.
  • 📐 Angle of Refraction – Angle between refracted ray and normal.
  • 🌍 Refractive Index – n = c/v.
