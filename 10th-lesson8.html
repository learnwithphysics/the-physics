📖 Introduction

Atoms combine with one another to form molecules and compounds. The force that holds atoms together is called a Chemical Bond.

🔗 Chemical Bond

A chemical bond is the attractive force that holds atoms together in a molecule or compound.

Examples

  • Water (H₂O)
  • Sodium Chloride (NaCl)
  • Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)

⚛ Octet Rule

Atoms tend to gain, lose or share electrons to achieve eight electrons in their outermost shell. This stable arrangement is called the Octet Rule.

Element Valence Electrons Action
Sodium 1 Loses 1 Electron
Chlorine 7 Gains 1 Electron
Oxygen 6 Needs 2 Electrons

⚡ Ionic Bond

An Ionic Bond is formed when one atom transfers electrons to another atom. This usually happens between a metal and a non-metal.

Example

Sodium (Na) loses one electron and becomes Na⁺.

Chlorine (Cl) gains one electron and becomes Cl⁻.

Both combine to form Sodium Chloride (NaCl).

🔗 Covalent Bond

A Covalent Bond is formed by sharing electrons between atoms. This usually occurs between non-metals.

Examples

  • Hydrogen (H₂)
  • Oxygen (O₂)
  • Water (H₂O)
  • Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)

⭐ Important Facts

  • ✅ Ionic Bond → Electron Transfer
  • ✅ Covalent Bond → Electron Sharing
  • ✅ Metals generally form Ionic Bonds.
  • ✅ Non-metals generally form Covalent Bonds.
  • ✅ NaCl is an ionic compound.
  • ✅ H₂O is a covalent compound.

📚 Improve Your Learning

1. What is a Chemical Bond?

A chemical bond is the attractive force that holds atoms together in a molecule or compound.

2. State the Octet Rule.

Atoms gain, lose or share electrons to achieve eight electrons in their outermost shell.

3. What is an Ionic Bond?

An ionic bond is formed by the transfer of electrons from one atom to another.

4. What is a Covalent Bond?

A covalent bond is formed by sharing electrons between atoms.

5. Give one example of an Ionic Compound.

Sodium Chloride (NaCl).

6. Give one example of a Covalent Compound.

Water (H₂O) or Carbon Dioxide (CO₂).

7. Why are Lewis Dot Structures used?

They show the valence electrons around atoms.

8. Which bond is formed between metals and non-metals?

Ionic Bond.

9. Which bond is formed between non-metals?

Covalent Bond.

10. Which bond involves electron sharing?

Covalent Bond.

⭐ Important Definitions

  • ⚛ Chemical Bond – Force that holds atoms together.
  • 🔗 Ionic Bond – Bond formed by electron transfer.
  • 🤝 Covalent Bond – Bond formed by electron sharing.
  • ⚡ Octet Rule – Atoms become stable by completing eight electrons.
  • 🧪 Lewis Dot Structure – Representation of valence electrons using dots.
