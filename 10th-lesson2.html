📖 Introduction

Every day we observe many changes around us. Milk turns into curd. Iron rusts. Food gets cooked. A candle burns. These changes happen because of chemical reactions. A chemical equation is a simple way to represent these reactions using symbols and chemical formulae.

🎯 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand chemical reactions.
  • Learn chemical equations.
  • Know reactants and products.
  • Identify different reaction types.
  • Perform virtual experiments.

⚗️ Chemical Reaction

A chemical reaction is a process in which one or more substances change into new substances having different properties.

Examples

  • 🔥 Burning of Magnesium Ribbon
  • 🍚 Cooking Food
  • 🧲 Rusting of Iron
  • 🥛 Milk changing into Curd

📝 Chemical Equation

A chemical equation represents a chemical reaction using symbols and formulae.

Example

2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

  • H₂ = Hydrogen
  • O₂ = Oxygen
  • H₂O = Water

🧪 Types of Chemical Reactions

Chemical reactions are classified into different types based on how reactants change into products.

Reaction Type General Form Example
Combination A + B → AB 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO
Decomposition AB → A + B CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂
Displacement A + BC → AC + B Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu
Double Displacement AB + CD → AD + CB Na₂SO₄ + BaCl₂ → BaSO₄ + 2NaCl

🟢 Combination Reaction

Two or more substances combine to form a single product.

Example

2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO

Magnesium burns in oxygen to form magnesium oxide.

🔴 Decomposition Reaction

One compound breaks into two or more simpler substances.

Example

CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂

Calcium carbonate decomposes on heating.

🟡 Displacement Reaction

A more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from its compound.

Example

Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu

🔵 Double Displacement Reaction

Two compounds exchange their ions to form two new compounds.

Example

Na₂SO₄ + BaCl₂ → BaSO₄ + 2NaCl

⭐ Important Points

  • ✅ Combination → One product is formed.
  • ✅ Decomposition → One compound breaks into simpler substances.
  • ✅ Displacement → More reactive element replaces a less reactive element.
  • ✅ Double Displacement → Exchange of ions takes place.

⚖ Balanced Chemical Equations

A balanced chemical equation has the same number of atoms of each element on both sides of the equation. This follows the Law of Conservation of Mass.

Balanced Equation

2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

Element Reactants Products
Hydrogen 4 4
Oxygen 2 2

❌ Unbalanced Chemical Equation

If the number of atoms is different on both sides, the equation is called an unbalanced chemical equation.

Example

H₂ + O₂ → H₂O

This equation is incorrect because oxygen atoms are not equal on both sides.

📚 Law of Conservation of Mass

Mass can neither be created nor destroyed during a chemical reaction. The total mass of reactants is always equal to the total mass of products.

Remember

Total atoms before reaction = Total atoms after reaction

📝 Steps to Balance Chemical Equations

  1. Write the correct chemical formulae.
  2. Count atoms on both sides.
  3. Add coefficients to balance atoms.
  4. Check every element again.
  5. Never change chemical formulae.

1. What is a chemical reaction?

A symbolic representation of a chemical reaction using chemical formulae.

The substances that take part in a chemical reaction.

The new substances formed after a chemical reaction.

Mass can neither be created nor destroyed during a chemical reaction.

Combination, Decomposition, Displacement and Double Displacement reactions.
