📖 Introduction

Every object in the universe attracts every other object. This attractive force is called Gravitational Force. It keeps planets moving around the Sun, the Moon around the Earth, and causes objects to fall towards Earth.

🎯 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand Gravitation.
  • Study Newton's Universal Law of Gravitation.
  • Learn Free Fall.
  • Differentiate Mass and Weight.
  • Perform Virtual Activities.

🌍 Gravitational Force

Gravitational force is the attractive force between any two objects having mass. The greater the masses, the greater the gravitational force. The greater the distance, the smaller the gravitational force.

Examples

  • 🍎 Apple falls from a tree.
  • 🌙 Moon revolves around Earth.
  • 🪐 Planets revolve around the Sun.

📘 Universal Law of Gravitation

Sir Isaac Newton stated that every object attracts every other object with a force directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

Formula

F = G × (m₁ × m₂) / r²

🍎 Free Fall

When an object falls towards the Earth only due to gravity, its motion is called Free Fall. In free fall, the object experiences Acceleration due to Gravity (g).

Acceleration due to Gravity

g = 9.8 m/s²

Near the Earth's surface, every freely falling object accelerates at approximately 9.8 m/s².

⚖ Mass

Mass is the amount of matter present in an object.

  • SI Unit = kilogram (kg)
  • Mass remains constant everywhere.
  • Measured using a Beam Balance.

🏋 Weight

Weight is the gravitational force acting on an object.

Formula

Weight = Mass × Gravity

W = m × g

  • SI Unit = Newton (N)
  • Measured using a Spring Balance.
  • Weight changes from place to place.
