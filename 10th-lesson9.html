📖 Introduction

Electricity is one of the most useful forms of energy. It is used in homes, industries, schools and hospitals. The movement of electric charges through a conductor is called Electric Current.

⚡ Electric Current

Electric current is the flow of electric charge through a conductor. Its SI unit is Ampere (A).

Formula

I = Q / t

I = Current
Q = Charge
t = Time

🔋 Electric Charge

Electric charge is the basic property of matter responsible for electrical effects. Its SI unit is Coulomb (C).

🔌 Electric Circuit

An electric circuit is a closed conducting path through which electric current flows.

  • 🔋 Battery
  • 💡 Bulb
  • 🔘 Switch
  • ➖ Connecting Wires

⚙ Conductors and Insulators

Materials that allow electric current to pass through them are called Conductors. Materials that do not allow current to pass are called Insulators.

Conductors Insulators
Copper Rubber
Aluminium Plastic
Silver Wood
Iron Glass

📖 Ohm's Law

Ohm's Law states that at constant temperature, the current flowing through a conductor is directly proportional to the potential difference across it.

V = IR

V = Voltage (Volt)
I = Current (Ampere)
R = Resistance (Ohm)

🧲 Resistance

Resistance is the property of a conductor that opposes the flow of electric current. Its SI unit is Ohm (Ω).

  • Long wire → More Resistance
  • Thick wire → Less Resistance
  • Copper → Low Resistance
  • Nichrome → High Resistance
