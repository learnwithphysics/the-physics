📖 Introduction

Everything around us is made of matter. Some materials contain only one kind of substance, while others contain two or more substances mixed together. Understanding the difference between pure substances and mixtures helps us choose suitable methods to separate them.

🎯 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand pure substances.
  • Differentiate pure substances and mixtures.
  • Learn different types of mixtures.
  • Know common separation methods.
  • Perform simple virtual activities.

🧪 Pure Substance

A pure substance contains only one type of particle. It has a fixed composition and definite properties.

Examples

  • 💧 Distilled Water
  • 🥇 Gold (Pure)
  • 🧂 Pure Salt
  • 💎 Diamond

🥣 Mixture

A mixture is formed when two or more substances are combined physically. The substances keep their own properties and can often be separated by physical methods.

Examples

  • 🌬 Air
  • 🧂 Salt Water
  • 🥗 Fruit Salad
  • 🥛 Milk

📘 Types of Mixtures

Mixtures are mainly classified into two types.

Homogeneous Mixture

The composition is uniform throughout the mixture.

Example: Salt solution.

Heterogeneous Mixture

The composition is not uniform. Different components can be seen separately.

Example: Sand and water.

🧪 Separation Methods

Different mixtures can be separated using different physical methods.

Common Methods

  • 🧲 Magnetic Separation
  • 🧹 Hand Picking
  • 🪣 Filtration
  • ☀ Evaporation
  • 💧 Sedimentation & Decantation
  • 🔥 Distillation
  • 🧻 Sublimation
  • 🌀 Centrifugation

📚 Improve Your Learning

1. What is a Pure Substance?

A pure substance contains only one kind of particle and has a fixed composition.

2. What is a Mixture?

A mixture contains two or more substances physically mixed together.

3. What is a Homogeneous Mixture?

A homogeneous mixture has a uniform composition throughout.

4. What is a Heterogeneous Mixture?

A heterogeneous mixture has a non-uniform composition.

5. Name four separation methods.

Filtration, Evaporation, Distillation and Magnetic Separation.

6. Which method separates sand from water?

Filtration.

7. Which method separates salt from salt water?

Evaporation.

8. Is air a pure substance?

No. Air is a homogeneous mixture of gases.

9. Give one example of a pure substance.

Distilled water, pure gold or diamond.

10. Give one example of a heterogeneous mixture.

Sand and water.

📖 Lesson Summary

  • ✅ Matter may be pure or mixed.
  • ✅ Pure substances contain only one type of particle.
  • ✅ Mixtures contain two or more substances.
  • ✅ Homogeneous mixtures have uniform composition.
  • ✅ Heterogeneous mixtures have non-uniform composition.
  • ✅ Mixtures can be separated by physical methods.
  • ✅ Filtration separates insoluble solids from liquids.
  • ✅ Evaporation separates dissolved solids from liquids.
