📖 Introduction

Everything around us is made of matter. The air we breathe, the water we drink, the food we eat, the books we read and our own body are all made of matter. In this lesson you will learn what matter is, its properties and its different states.

1. What is Matter?

Anything which has mass and occupies space is called Matter. Examples:

  • 💧 Water
  • 🌬 Air
  • 🪨 Stone
  • 📖 Book
  • 🪵 Wood
  • 🔩 Iron

2. Physical Nature of Matter

Matter is made up of very tiny particles. These particles are so small that we cannot see them with our naked eyes. Every object around us is formed by millions of tiny particles.

💡 Did You Know?

A single drop of water contains millions of tiny particles called molecules.

3. Characteristics of Particles of Matter

Particles of matter have the following characteristics:

🔹 1. Particles are very small

Matter is made up of extremely tiny particles which cannot be seen with our naked eyes.

🔹 2. There is space between particles

The particles of matter have spaces between them. Because of this, one substance can mix with another.

🔹 3. Particles are always moving

Particles are in continuous motion. Their movement increases when temperature increases.

🔹 4. Particles attract each other

Particles attract one another. The force of attraction is strongest in solids, less in liquids and weakest in gases.

4. States of Matter

Matter exists mainly in three states. Each state has different properties.

🧊 Solid

• Has a fixed shape.
• Has a fixed volume.
• Particles are tightly packed.
• Example: Stone, Iron, Wood.

💧 Liquid

• Has no fixed shape.
• Has a fixed volume.
• Takes the shape of its container.
• Example: Water, Milk, Oil.

💨 Gas

• Has no fixed shape.
• Has no fixed volume.
• Particles move freely in all directions.
• Example: Air, Oxygen, Carbon dioxide.

5. Change of State of Matter

Matter can change from one state to another by changing temperature or pressure.

🧊 ➜ 💧 Melting

A solid changes into a liquid by heating. Example: Ice melts into water.

💧 ➜ 💨 Boiling / Evaporation

A liquid changes into a gas by heating. Example: Water changes into steam.

💨 ➜ 💧 Condensation

A gas changes into a liquid by cooling. Example: Water vapour forms water droplets.

💧 ➜ 🧊 Freezing

A liquid changes into a solid by cooling. Example: Water changes into ice.

6. Diffusion

Diffusion is the process in which particles of one substance mix with the particles of another substance on their own. This happens because the particles of matter are always moving.

🌸 Example 1

When a perfume bottle is opened, its smell spreads throughout the room. This is called diffusion.

☕ Example 2

When sugar is added to water, it spreads throughout the water without continuous stirring.

🌡️ Important Point

Diffusion takes place faster in gases, slower in liquids, and slowest in solids because the movement of particles is different in each state.

🧪 Activity

Activity 1 – Observe Diffusion

Materials Required: Water, a glass, and a few drops of ink or food colour.

Procedure

  1. Take a glass filled with clean water.
  2. Add one or two drops of ink or food colour.
  3. Do not stir the water.
  4. Observe what happens for a few minutes.

Observation

The colour slowly spreads throughout the water without stirring.

Conclusion

This shows that particles of matter are always moving. This process is called Diffusion.
