📖 Introduction

Atoms are extremely small building blocks of matter. Scientists discovered that atoms contain even smaller particles. Learning about these particles helps us understand the structure of matter.

⚛ Structure of an Atom

An atom has a central nucleus surrounded by electrons. The nucleus contains protons and neutrons.

🔬 Subatomic Particles

  • ➕ Proton
  • ⚪ Neutron
  • ➖ Electron

🎯 Nucleus

The nucleus is the central part of an atom. It contains protons and neutrons. Almost all the mass of an atom is concentrated in the nucleus.

Contains

  • ➕ Protons (Positive charge)
  • ⚪ Neutrons (No charge)

⚡ Electrons

Electrons are very small negatively charged particles. They move around the nucleus in energy levels (shells).

Electron Facts

  • Negative charge
  • Very small mass
  • Move around the nucleus

📚 Improve Your Learning

1. What is an atom?

An atom is the basic building block of matter.

2. What is the nucleus?

The nucleus is the central part of an atom containing protons and neutrons.

3. Which particle has a positive charge?

Proton.

4. Which particle has a negative charge?

Electron.

5. Which particle has no charge?

Neutron.

6. Where are protons found?

Inside the nucleus.

7. Where do electrons move?

Around the nucleus in energy levels (shells).

8. Which part of an atom contains most of its mass?

The nucleus.

9. Name the three subatomic particles.

Protons, Neutrons and Electrons.

10. Why is the atom important?

Atoms combine to form all substances around us.

📖 Lesson Summary

  • ✅ Matter is made up of atoms.
  • ✅ Atoms contain protons, neutrons and electrons.
  • ✅ The nucleus contains protons and neutrons.
  • ✅ Electrons move around the nucleus.
  • ✅ Protons are positive, electrons are negative and neutrons have no charge.
