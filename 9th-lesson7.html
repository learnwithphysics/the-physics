📖 Introduction

Everything around us is made of tiny particles. These particles combine in different ways to form substances and participate in chemical reactions.

🎯 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand atoms and molecules.
  • Learn chemical reactions.
  • Understand chemical equations.
  • Observe simple virtual experiments.
  • Revise through summary.

⚛ Atom

An atom is the smallest particle of an element that retains its chemical properties.

Examples

  • Hydrogen (H)
  • Oxygen (O)
  • Carbon (C)
  • Iron (Fe)

🧬 Molecule

A molecule is formed when two or more atoms combine chemically.

Examples

  • H₂O - Water
  • O₂ - Oxygen
  • CO₂ - Carbon Dioxide
  • NH₃ - Ammonia

🧪 Chemical Reaction

A chemical reaction is a process in which reactants change into new substances called products.

Examples

  • Burning of paper
  • Rusting of iron
  • Cooking food
  • Digestion of food

📝 Chemical Equation

A chemical equation represents a chemical reaction using symbols and chemical formulas.

Example

2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

🔬 Practical Activity

Mix vinegar and baking soda carefully. Observe what happens.

Observation

Bubbles of carbon dioxide gas are produced. This shows that a chemical reaction has taken place.

✨ Characteristics of a Chemical Reaction

  • 🔥 Change in temperature
  • 🎨 Change in colour
  • 💨 Gas may be released
  • 🌧 A precipitate may form
  • 💡 Sometimes light is produced

📚 Improve Your Learning

1. What is an Atom?

An atom is the smallest particle of an element that retains its chemical properties.

2. What is a Molecule?

A molecule is formed when two or more atoms combine chemically.

3. What is a Chemical Reaction?

A chemical reaction is a process in which reactants change into products.

4. What are Reactants?

Reactants are the substances present before a chemical reaction starts.

5. What are Products?

Products are the new substances formed after a chemical reaction.

6. Write one Chemical Equation.

2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

7. Name two Chemical Reactions.

Rusting of iron and burning of paper.

8. Mention two signs of a Chemical Reaction.

Colour change and gas evolution.

9. Is cooking food a Chemical Reaction?

Yes. New substances are formed during cooking.

10. Give one example of a Molecule.

Water (H₂O).
