📖 Introduction

Motion means change in the position of an object with time. Everything around us is either at rest or in motion. Cars, trains, birds, planets and even the Earth are in motion.

🎯 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand Motion.
  • Learn Relative Motion.
  • Know Distance and Displacement.
  • Understand Speed and Velocity.
  • Perform Virtual Activities.

🌍 Relative Motion

Motion depends on the observer. The same object may appear moving for one person and at rest for another.

Relative Motion

Example

A passenger sitting inside a moving bus feels other passengers are at rest. But a person standing outside sees the whole bus moving.

📏 Distance and Displacement

Distance is the total path travelled. Displacement is the shortest distance between starting and ending positions.

Distance vs Displacement

Remember

  • Distance → Scalar Quantity
  • Displacement → Vector Quantity
  • SI Unit → metre (m)

🚗 Speed and Velocity

Speed tells how fast an object moves. Velocity tells both speed and direction.

Speed vs Velocity

Difference

  • Speed uses Distance.
  • Velocity uses Displacement.
  • SI Unit = m/s.

🚶 Uniform Motion

When an object covers equal distances in equal intervals of time, it is called Uniform Motion.

Example

A train moving continuously at 60 km/h on a straight track.

Uniform Motion

🏃 Non-Uniform Motion

When an object covers unequal distances in equal intervals of time, or equal distances in unequal intervals of time, it is called Non-Uniform Motion.

Example

A bike moving in city traffic.

Non-Uniform Motion

⚡ Acceleration

Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity with time.

Formula

Acceleration = Change in Velocity ÷ Time

SI Unit = m/s²

Acceleration

📘 Equations of Motion

Equation 1

v = u + at

Equation 2

s = ut + ½at²

Equation 3

v² = u² + 2as

📝 Solved Example

A car starts from rest with an acceleration of 2 m/s² for 5 seconds. Find the final velocity.

Answer

u = 0 m/s
a = 2 m/s²
t = 5 s

v = u + at
v = 0 + (2 × 5)

v = 10 m/s
