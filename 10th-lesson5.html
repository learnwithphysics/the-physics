📖 Introduction

The human eye is one of the most important sense organs. It enables us to see objects, colours and movements around us. The eye works like a natural camera.

🎯 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the structure of the human eye.
  • Learn the functions of eye parts.
  • Understand accommodation.
  • Know common eye defects.
  • Learn about the colourful world.

👁 Human Eye

The human eye collects light and forms a real, inverted image on the retina. The brain processes this image, allowing us to see objects clearly.

Main Parts

  • 👁 Cornea
  • ⚫ Pupil
  • 🎨 Iris
  • 🔍 Eye Lens
  • 🟠 Retina
  • 🧠 Optic Nerve

📚 Functions of Eye Parts

Part Function
Cornea Allows light to enter the eye.
Iris Controls the size of the pupil.
Pupil Regulates the amount of light entering.
Eye Lens Focuses light on the retina.
Retina Forms the image.
Optic Nerve Sends signals to the brain.

🔍 Power of Accommodation

The eye lens changes its focal length to focus both nearby and distant objects clearly. This ability is called the Power of Accommodation.

Near Object

Lens becomes thicker.

Distant Object

Lens becomes thinner.

👓 Defects of Vision

Sometimes the eye cannot focus images properly on the retina. This causes defects of vision.

Defect Problem Correction
Myopia Cannot see distant objects clearly. Concave Lens
Hypermetropia Cannot see nearby objects clearly. Convex Lens
Presbyopia Difficulty seeing nearby objects due to ageing. Bifocal Lens

🔍 Myopia (Short Sight)

A person with myopia can see nearby objects clearly but distant objects appear blurred.

Correction

A Concave Lens diverges light rays and helps focus the image on the retina.

🔎 Hypermetropia (Long Sight)

A person with hypermetropia can see distant objects clearly but nearby objects appear blurred.

Correction

A Convex Lens converges light rays and forms the image on the retina.

👴 Presbyopia

Presbyopia occurs in old age because the eye lens loses its flexibility.

Correction

Bifocal lenses are used to correct presbyopia.

🌈 Dispersion of Light

When white light passes through a prism, it splits into seven colours. This phenomenon is called Dispersion of Light.

VIBGYOR

Violet • Indigo • Blue • Green • Yellow • Orange • Red

🌈 Formation of Rainbow

A rainbow is formed due to the refraction, dispersion and internal reflection of sunlight inside water droplets.

Conditions

  • ☀ Sun should be behind the observer.
  • 🌧 Water droplets should be in front.
  • 🌈 Seven colours become visible.

⭐ Important Facts

  • ✅ Normal eye near point = 25 cm.
  • ✅ Retina is the image-forming screen.
  • ✅ Iris controls the pupil.
  • ✅ Optic nerve sends signals to the brain.
  • ✅ Rainbow has seven colours (VIBGYOR).

📚 Improve Your Learning

1. Which part of the eye controls the amount of light entering the eye?

The Iris controls the size of the pupil and regulates the amount of light entering the eye.

2. Which part of the eye forms the image?

The Retina forms the real and inverted image.

3. What is the power of accommodation?

The ability of the eye lens to change its focal length and focus both near and distant objects.

4. Which lens corrects Myopia?

Concave Lens.

5. Which lens corrects Hypermetropia?

Convex Lens.

6. Which lens corrects Presbyopia?

Bifocal Lens.

7. What is dispersion of light?

The splitting of white light into seven colours is called dispersion.

8. Name the seven colours of sunlight.

Violet, Indigo, Blue, Green, Yellow, Orange and Red (VIBGYOR).

9. How is a rainbow formed?

A rainbow is formed due to refraction, dispersion and internal reflection of sunlight inside water droplets.

10. What is the near point of a normal eye?

25 cm.

⭐ Important Definitions

  • 👁 Retina → Image forming screen.
  • 🔍 Accommodation → Change in focal length of eye lens.
  • 👓 Myopia → Near objects clear, distant objects blurred.
  • 👓 Hypermetropia → Distant objects clear, nearby objects blurred.
  • 🌈 Dispersion → Splitting of white light into seven colours.
  • 🌦 Rainbow → Natural spectrum formed by water droplets.
