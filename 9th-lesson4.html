📖 Introduction

Light always travels in a straight line in the same medium. When light travels from one transparent medium to another, its direction changes. This bending of light is called Refraction.

🎯 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand refraction of light.
  • Know transparent media.
  • Differentiate denser and rarer media.
  • Learn the laws of refraction.
  • Understand refractive index.
  • Observe virtual experiments.

💡 Light

Light is a form of energy that enables us to see objects. It travels in a straight line in a uniform medium.

Examples

  • ☀ Sun
  • 💡 Electric Bulb
  • 🔥 Candle
  • 🔦 Torch

🔍 Refraction of Light

When a ray of light passes from one transparent medium to another transparent medium, it bends at the boundary. This phenomenon is called Refraction of Light.

Examples

  • 🥄 Spoon appears bent in water.
  • 🪙 Coin appears raised in water.
  • 🏊 Swimming pool appears shallower.

🌊 Denser and Rarer Medium

The speed of light changes when it travels from one medium to another. A medium in which light travels more slowly is called an Optically Denser Medium. A medium in which light travels faster is called an Optically Rarer Medium.

Examples

  • Air → Optically Rarer
  • Water → Denser than Air
  • Glass → Denser than Water
  • Diamond → One of the Densest Optical Media

📏 Laws of Refraction

Refraction follows two important laws.

First Law

The incident ray, the refracted ray and the normal lie in the same plane.

Second Law (Snell's Law)

For a given pair of media, the ratio sin i / sin r remains constant.

📘 Refractive Index

Refractive Index tells us how much light bends while entering another medium.

Formula

n = Speed of Light in Vacuum / Speed of Light in Medium

Examples

  • Air ≈ 1.0003
  • Water ≈ 1.33
  • Glass ≈ 1.5
  • Diamond ≈ 2.42

🧪 Practical Activity

Take a glass tumbler and fill it with clean water. Place a pencil inside the water. Observe carefully.

Observation

The pencil appears bent at the water surface because of refraction of light.
