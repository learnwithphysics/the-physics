📖 Introduction

Carbon is one of the most important elements found in nature. All living organisms contain carbon compounds. Carbon forms millions of compounds because of its special properties.

  • Understand Carbon.
  • Know Covalent Bonding.

  • Study Allotropes.
  • Know Carbon Compounds.

⚫ Carbon

Carbon has Atomic Number 6. It has four valence electrons. Therefore it forms four covalent bonds.

Property Value
Symbol C
Atomic Number 6
Valency 4

🔗 Catenation

The ability of carbon atoms to join together and form long chains is called Catenation.

Examples

C-C
C-C-C
C-C-C-C
C-C-C-C-C

💎 Allotropes of Carbon

The different forms of the same element having different physical properties are called Allotropes.

Allotrope Property Uses
💎 Diamond Hardest natural substance Jewellery, Cutting Tools
✏ Graphite Soft, Conducts Electricity Pencils, Electrodes
⚽ Fullerene Football-shaped Molecule Nanotechnology

⛽ Hydrocarbons

Compounds containing only Carbon and Hydrogen are called Hydrocarbons.

Examples

  • Methane (CH₄)
  • Ethane (C₂H₆)
  • Propane (C₃H₈)
  • Butane (C₄H₁₀)

🟢 Saturated Compounds

Compounds having only single bonds between carbon atoms are called Saturated Compounds.

Example: CH₄
C₂H₆

🔴 Unsaturated Compounds

Compounds having double or triple bonds between carbon atoms.

Examples

C₂H₄ (Double Bond)
C₂H₂ (Triple Bond)

📊 Saturated vs Unsaturated

Saturated Unsaturated
Single Bond Double / Triple Bond
Less Reactive More Reactive
Alkanes Alkenes / Alkynes

🧪 Functional Groups

An atom or group of atoms that determines the chemical properties of an organic compound is called a Functional Group.

Functional Group Formula Example
Alcohol -OH Ethanol
Carboxylic Acid -COOH Ethanoic Acid
Aldehyde -CHO Methanal
Ketone >C=O Propanone

🍶 Ethanol

Ethanol is an alcohol having the molecular formula C₂H₅OH. It is colourless, volatile and widely used in medicines, perfumes and sanitizers.

Uses

  • Medicines
  • Perfumes
  • Hand Sanitizers
  • Laboratories

🧴 Ethanoic Acid

Ethanoic Acid (Acetic Acid) has the molecular formula CH₃COOH. It is the main component of vinegar.

Uses

  • Vinegar
  • Food Preservation
  • Chemical Industry

🧼 Soaps and Detergents

Soaps and detergents are cleansing agents used to remove dirt and oil.

Soap Detergent
Works well in Soft Water Works in Hard & Soft Water
Biodegradable Some are Non-biodegradable
Forms Scum in Hard Water No Scum Formation

📚 Improve Your Learning

1. What is Catenation?

The property of carbon to form long chains by bonding with other carbon atoms.

2. What are Allotropes?

Different forms of the same element having different physical properties.

3. Name three allotropes of Carbon.

Diamond, Graphite and Fullerene.

4. Why is Diamond hard?

Because each carbon atom is strongly bonded to four other carbon atoms.

5. Why does Graphite conduct electricity?

Because it contains free electrons that can move easily.

6. What are Hydrocarbons?

Compounds made only of Carbon and Hydrogen.

7. What is Ethanol?

An alcohol with the formula C₂H₅OH.

8. What is Ethanoic Acid?

An acid with the formula CH₃COOH, commonly called Acetic Acid.

9. What is a Functional Group?

An atom or group of atoms responsible for the characteristic chemical properties of a compound.

10. Difference between Soap and Detergent?

Soap forms scum in hard water while detergents work in both hard and soft water.

⭐ Important Definitions

  • ⚫ Carbon – Element with atomic number 6.
  • 🔗 Catenation – Carbon forms long chains.
  • 💎 Allotropes – Different forms of the same element.
  • 🧪 Functional Group – Determines chemical properties.
  • 🍶 Ethanol – Alcohol (C₂H₅OH).
  • 🧴 Ethanoic Acid – Acetic Acid (CH₃COOH).
