📖 Introduction

Why do ships float while a small stone sinks? This lesson explains how liquids exert an upward force and how density affects floating.

🌊 Buoyant Force

A liquid pushes upward on objects placed inside it. This upward push is called Buoyant Force.

⚓ Floating and Sinking

Objects with lower average density than the liquid usually float. Objects with higher average density usually sink.

⚖ Density

Density tells us how much mass is present in a given volume. Comparing densities helps explain why different objects behave differently in water.

🧪 Virtual Lab - Floating Test

Select an object and observe whether it floats or sinks.

⚪

🔬 Practical Activity

Take a bowl of water. Try placing different objects such as a coin, plastic bottle cap, wooden stick and sponge into the water. Observe which objects float and which sink.

Observation

Different objects behave differently depending on their properties and the liquid they are placed in.

📚 Improve Your Learning

Why does a wooden block usually float?

A wooden block experiences enough upward buoyant force to remain on the surface.

Why does a stone usually sink?

A stone sinks because the upward buoyant force is not enough to keep it floating.

📖 Lesson Summary

  • ✅ Liquids exert an upward force on objects.
  • ✅ Some objects float while others sink.
  • ✅ Floating depends on the properties of the object and the liquid.
  • ✅ Simple experiments help us understand buoyancy.
