📖 Introduction

Many substances we use every day are acidic or basic. Lemon juice, vinegar and tamarind are acids. Soap solution and baking soda are bases. When an acid reacts with a base, a salt is formed.

🎯 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand acids.
  • Understand bases.
  • Understand salts.
  • Learn indicators.
  • Perform virtual activities.

🍋 Acids

Acids are substances that produce hydrogen ions (H⁺) in aqueous solution. They usually have a sour taste.

Examples

  • 🍋 Lemon Juice
  • 🍊 Orange Juice
  • 🧴 Vinegar
  • 🥭 Tamarind

🧼 Bases

Bases produce hydroxide ions (OH⁻) in water. They are bitter in taste and feel slippery.

Examples

  • 🧼 Soap Solution
  • 🪥 Toothpaste
  • 🥛 Lime Water
  • 🧂 Baking Soda

🧂 Salts

Salts are formed when an acid reacts with a base. This reaction is called Neutralization.

Example

HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O

🎨 Indicators

Indicators help us identify whether a substance is acidic or basic.

Indicator Acid Base
Litmus Paper Blue → Red Red → Blue
Turmeric Yellow Reddish Brown
Phenolphthalein Colourless Pink

🌈 pH Scale

The pH scale measures how acidic or basic a solution is. Its values range from 0 to 14.

pH Value Nature Examples
0 – 6 Acidic Lemon Juice, Vinegar
7 Neutral Pure Water
8 – 14 Basic Soap Solution, Lime Water

⚗ Strong and Weak Acids & Bases

Type Examples
Strong Acids HCl, H₂SO₄, HNO₃
Weak Acids CH₃COOH, Citric Acid
Strong Bases NaOH, KOH
Weak Bases NH₄OH

⚖ Neutralization Reaction

When an acid reacts with a base, salt and water are formed.

General Equation

Acid + Base → Salt + Water


Example

HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O

🌍 Uses in Daily Life

  • 🍋 Lemon contains citric acid.
  • 🧼 Soap is basic.
  • 🦷 Toothpaste is slightly basic.
  • 💊 Antacids neutralize excess stomach acid.
  • 🌱 Farmers add lime to acidic soil.

📚 Improve Your Learning

1. What is an acid?

An acid is a substance that produces hydrogen ions (H⁺) in aqueous solution.

2. What is a base?

A base is a substance that produces hydroxide ions (OH⁻) in water.

3. What is a salt?

A salt is formed when an acid reacts with a base.

4. What is neutralization?

The reaction between an acid and a base to form salt and water is called neutralization.

5. What is the pH of pure water?

The pH of pure water is 7.

6. Name two natural indicators.

Turmeric and China Rose indicator.

7. Which acid is present in lemon?

Citric Acid.

8. Which acid is present in vinegar?

Acetic Acid.

9. Why is toothpaste basic?

It neutralizes the acids present in the mouth.

10. Give two uses of acids.

Manufacturing fertilizers and car batteries.

⭐ Important Definitions

  • 🍋 Acid → Produces H⁺ ions.
  • 🧼 Base → Produces OH⁻ ions.
  • 🧂 Salt → Product of acid + base reaction.
  • ⚖ Neutralization → Acid + Base → Salt + Water.
  • 🌈 pH Scale → Measures acidity or basicity.
