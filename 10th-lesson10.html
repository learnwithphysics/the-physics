📖 Introduction

A magnet is an object that attracts iron, nickel and cobalt. The branch of science that studies electricity and magnetism together is called Electromagnetism.

🎯 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand magnets.
  • Know magnetic poles.
  • Study magnetic field.
  • Learn electromagnets.
  • Perform virtual experiments.

🧲 Magnet

A magnet always has two poles.

Pole Meaning
North Pole N
South Pole S

Like poles repel each other. Unlike poles attract each other.

🌍 Magnetic Field

The region around a magnet where magnetic force can be experienced is called the Magnetic Field.

  • Field lines start from North Pole.
  • Field lines end at South Pole.
  • Closer field lines → Strong magnetic field.

🧪 Activity

Which poles attract each other?

🧲 Magnetic Field Lines

Magnetic field lines are imaginary lines that represent the direction and strength of the magnetic field.

  • ➡ Field lines start from the North Pole.
  • ➡ Field lines end at the South Pole.
  • ➡ They never intersect each other.
  • ➡ Closer lines indicate a stronger magnetic field.

⚡ Oersted's Experiment

Hans Christian Oersted discovered that an electric current produces a magnetic field around a conductor. This experiment proved the relationship between electricity and magnetism.

Observation

When current flows through a wire, a nearby compass needle gets deflected.

🧲 Electromagnet

An electromagnet is a temporary magnet produced by passing electric current through a coil wound around a soft iron core.

Permanent Magnet Electromagnet
Always magnetic Magnetic only when current flows
Cannot be switched OFF Can be switched ON/OFF
Fixed strength Strength can be changed

  • 👍 Thumb → Direction of Current
  • 👉 Curved Fingers → Direction of Magnetic Field

⚙ Electric Motor

An electric motor is a device that converts electrical energy into mechanical energy. It works on the principle that a current-carrying conductor placed in a magnetic field experiences a force.

Main Parts

  • 🧲 Magnet
  • 🌀 Coil
  • 🔋 Battery
  • ⚫ Split Ring
  • 🔩 Brushes

⚡ Electromagnetic Induction

Electromagnetic induction is the process of producing electric current by moving a conductor inside a magnetic field. It was discovered by Michael Faraday.

Key Idea

Move Magnet ➜ Current Produced

No Movement ➜ No Current

🔋 Electric Generator

An electric generator converts mechanical energy into electrical energy. Generators work on the principle of electromagnetic induction.

Motor Generator
Electrical → Mechanical Mechanical → Electrical
Consumes Electricity Produces Electricity

⭐ Important Definitions

  • 🧲 Magnet – Material that attracts iron, nickel and cobalt.
  • 🌍 Magnetic Field – Region around a magnet where force acts.
  • ⚡ Electromagnet – Temporary magnet produced using electric current.
  • 🔄 Electromagnetic Induction – Generation of current by moving a conductor in a magnetic field.
  • ⚙ Electric Motor – Converts Electrical Energy → Mechanical Energy.
  • 🔋 Electric Generator – Converts Mechanical Energy → Electrical Energy.
