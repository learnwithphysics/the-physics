📚 Introduction

More than 100 elements are known today. Scientists classified these elements based on their properties so that they could be studied easily. This arrangement is called the Periodic Table.

🎯 Learning Outcomes

  • Understand why elements are classified.
  • Know the Modern Periodic Table.
  • Learn Groups and Periods.
  • Study metals, non-metals and metalloids.
  • Understand periodic properties.

📖 What is the Periodic Table?

The Periodic Table is a systematic arrangement of elements in increasing order of their atomic numbers. Elements having similar chemical properties are placed in the same group.

📖 Groups and Periods

The Modern Periodic Table is divided into vertical columns called Groups and horizontal rows called Periods. Elements in the same group have similar chemical properties because they have the same number of valence electrons.

Groups Periods
18 Vertical Columns 7 Horizontal Rows
Similar Properties Gradual Change in Properties

⚙ Metals, Non-metals & Metalloids

Type Properties
Metals Good conductors of heat and electricity
Non-metals Poor conductors, brittle
Metalloids Show properties of both metals and non-metals

📋 Examples

Category Examples
Metals Iron, Copper, Aluminium
Non-metals Oxygen, Nitrogen, Sulphur
Metalloids Silicon, Germanium

📈 Periodic Trends

  • ➡ Atomic size generally decreases across a period.
  • ⬇ Atomic size generally increases down a group.
  • ➡ Metallic character decreases across a period.
  • ⬇ Metallic character increases down a group.

Group 18 Noble Gases Helium, Neon, Argon

⚛ Valency

Valency is the combining capacity of an element. It depends on the number of electrons present in the outermost shell.

Element Valence Electrons Valency
Hydrogen 1 1
Carbon 4 4
Oxygen 6 2
Sodium 1 1
Chlorine 7 1

📚 Improve Your Learning

1. Why do we classify elements?

Elements are classified to make their study easy and to understand their properties systematically.

2. What is the Modern Periodic Table?

It is the arrangement of elements in increasing order of atomic number.

3. How many groups are present?

18 Groups.

4. How many periods are present?

7 Periods.

5. What are Alkali Metals?

Group 1 elements are called Alkali Metals.

6. Which group contains Halogens?

Group 17.

7. Which group contains Noble Gases?

Group 18.

8. What is Valency?

Valency is the combining capacity of an element.

9. Give one example of a Metalloid.

Silicon.

10. On what basis are elements arranged?

They are arranged in increasing order of Atomic Number.

⭐ Important Definitions

  • 📖 Periodic Table – Arrangement of elements in increasing atomic number.
  • 📏 Group – Vertical column.
  • 📐 Period – Horizontal row.
  • ⚛ Valency – Combining capacity of an element.
  • 🧪 Metalloid – Element having properties of both metals and non-metals.
